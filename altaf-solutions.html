Project stories

Three times the work changed something.

The shape of each problem, how it got taken apart, and what the outcome looked like.

ROI Prioritisation

Turned six months of feature requests into a ranked delivery roadmap.

Situation
A backlog with 2,000+ JIRA items and no criteria. Engineering effort going to whoever asked loudest. Six months of accumulated requests, no way to separate signal from noise.
Approach
Every request audited. Grouped by functional impact and manual effort saved. Ten stakeholder interviews to validate which items actually moved the business. A scoring model built from real ROI, not opinion.
Outcome
Engineering effort realigned to the top 20% of items driving 80% of business value. Prioritisation model became repeatable across future cycles.
80% of value from 20% of backlog
Process Redesign

Rebuilt a 72-day delivery cycle down to 42 days.

Situation
A multi-team delivery cycle averaging 72 days. Handoff failures between 12 teams. No visibility into where work was getting stuck or why deadlines kept slipping.
Approach
Every handoff mapped. Six structural failure points identified. Flow redesigned around those failure points — not around the org chart. Visibility built into each stage so blockers surfaced in real time.
Outcome
Cycle time from 72 to 42 days. Teams stopped working around the same bottlenecks. The redesign became the standard process across all delivery programs.
42% faster delivery
Automation Program

Freed 25% of team capacity without adding a single hire.

Situation
A growing operation where manual work was compounding faster than the team could absorb. Hiring more wasn't the solution — the underlying workflows were broken.
Approach
Workflows documented end to end. Tasks ranked by hours consumed vs. business impact. Automation specs written, output validated, results measured. Program ran in phases with clear ROI at each stage.
Outcome
25% of team capacity freed for high-value work. Automation program became self-sustaining with a repeatable model for identifying the next round.
25% more capacity, same team

For startup teams

Direct answers to the questions founders actually ask.

Why not hire a PM and an ops lead separately?
Because at the stage where both are needed, the work bleeds between them constantly. Someone who holds both keeps the team from losing context every time a decision crosses the line.
What does the first 90 days look like?
Weeks 1–2: understand how work actually flows and where it breaks. Weeks 3–4: structure and visibility built in. From there: delivery rhythm, automation backlog, and the first round of process improvements.
What kind of environment works best here?
Fast-moving, under-defined, and high-stakes. "We're growing but things keep breaking" is the ideal starting point. A fully-staffed, perfectly organised company doesn't need this kind of work.
What makes this different from a consultant?
Consultants recommend. This work ships. The deliverable isn't a deck — it's a working system, a delivered roadmap, a team that knows how to run the process without being managed through it every cycle.
